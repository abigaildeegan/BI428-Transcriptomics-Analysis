## KEAP1–NRF2 Pathway Bioinformatics: Links to Sleep Disturbances in Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar Disorder (BD) is a complex neuropsychiatric disorder which has a lifetime prevalence of ~1% and is characterised by depressive and manic episodes. 

The goal of this analysis is to find biological underpinnings that will help us understand and treat BD. 

Genetic variations in the KEAP1-NRF2 oxidative stress pathway is believed to contribute to sleep disturbances in BD.

Insomnia is a sleep disorder which is highly heritable and is associated with difficulties remaining and falling asleep. 

Publicly available genome-wide association studies (GWAS) for BD and insomnia as well as expression quantitative trait loci (eQTL) datasets will 
be utilised to identify gene expression and single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) in the KEAP1-NRF2 pathway, brain tissues and colocalization analysis.
